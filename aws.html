Cloud Computing: Technical Foundations

NIST Definition (SP 800-145)

"Cloud computing is a model for enabling ubiquitous, convenient, on-demand network access to a shared pool of configurable computing resources (e.g., networks, servers, storage, applications, and services) that can be rapidly provisioned and released with minimal management effort or service provider interaction."

On-Demand Self-Service

Provision resources as needed without human interaction from AWS.

Resource Pooling

Multi-tenant model where resources are dynamically assigned.

The "Utility" Analogy

Think of AWS like MERALCO. You don't build a power plant in your backyard; you plug into the grid. You pay only for the kilowatts you consume.

  • No Long-term Commitments (usually)
  • Elasticity: Automatic growth/shrinkage
  • Measured Service: Transparent billing

6 Advantages: Detailed Business Case

CapEx to OpEx

Shift Capital Expenditure (Servers/Data Centers) to Operational Expenditure (Monthly usage). No heavy upfront investment.

Economies of Scale

AWS aggregates millions of customers. This allows them to buy hardware at a scale that significantly lowers the cost for you.

Stop Guessing Capacity

Stop buying hardware for "Peak Load." Cloud allows you to scale up exactly when the load hits and down when it's gone.

Speed & Agility

Reducing the time to deploy resources from weeks (procurement) to minutes (API call). Experiment faster.

Focus on Business

Stop "undifferentiated heavy lifting" like racking and stacking servers or managing cooling. Focus on your application code.

Go Global in Minutes

Deploy your application in multiple regions around the world with just a few clicks. Lower latency for your global users.

AWS Global Infrastructure: Architecture Levels

Regions

Geographic locations with 3+ isolated Availability Zones.

AZs

Isolated data centers within a region, connected via low-latency fiber.

Edge Locations

Endpoints used to cache content via CloudFront (CDN).

Local Zones

Putting compute/storage close to populous centers (like Manila).

Technical Metric:

AZs are typically separated by miles (tens of kilometers) to protect against concurrent disasters (fire/flood) while maintaining millisecond latencies.

Shared Responsibility Model: The Deep Reality

Responsibility shifts depending on the type of service you use (IaaS vs PaaS vs SaaS).

Customer: Security "IN" the Cloud

  • Customer Data: Encryption & Integrity.
  • Platform & IAM: Who has access? (Identity/Access).
  • Network & Firewall: Security Groups, ACLs, VPC.
  • Server-Side: Patching the Guest OS (Windows/Linux).

AWS: Security "OF" the Cloud

  • Physical Facilities: Biometrics, Perimeter security.
  • Hardware: Servers, Disks, Wiring.
  • Hypervisor: The virtualization software layer.
  • Networking: Global backbone fiber.

Account Safety: The Root User Lockdown

The Danger of "Root"

The Root User is the identity created when you first sign up. It has absolute, irrevocable power over every resource and billing detail.

Warning

Never use Root for daily work. If Root is compromised, your whole company is gone.

Resolution

Create an IAM User with "AdministratorAccess" for yourself.

The Root Protocol

  1. Log in only ONCE to set up.
  2. Activate MFA (Non-negotiable).
  3. Create Admin IAM User.
  4. Securely store Root credentials.
  5. DON'T LOG IN AGAIN.
SafetySlide 12/32

IAM: The Identity Firewall

Users

Individual people (you, your colleagues) or applications.

Groups

Collections of Users that share the same permissions (e.g. "Devs", "Admins").

Roles

Temporary permissions given to AWS Services or external entities.

The Principle of Least Privilege (POLP)

Never give a user more power than they strictly need. "Don't give the Janitor the keys to the Vault."

IAMSlide 13/32

The Shield: Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA)

Why MFA?

Passwords are "Something you KNOW." MFA adds "Something you HAVE." Even with your password, a hacker cannot enter without your device.

Virtual MFA Apps (Google/Authy)
Hardware Security Keys (YubiKey)

First Task in the Console

"Activate MFA on your Root user immediately after account verification."

Governance: AWS Budgets

The Student Defense

Cloud bills can spiral if you forget a resource. AWS Budgets proactively alerts you before you spend money.

Recommended Strategy

  • Set a Budget of $1.00 USD.
  • Alert at 80% Actual Spend ($0.80).
  • Alert at 100% Forecasted Spend.

How it works:

AWS checks your usage multiple times per day. If the math predicts you'll hit your budget, an email/SNS notification is triggered instantly.

Subject: AWS Budget Alert
"You have exceeded 80% of your $1 budget..."
